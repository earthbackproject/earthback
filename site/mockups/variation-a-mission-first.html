From Soil to Shelter

1.8 billion people lack adequate housing. We're changing that.

Through hemp construction, 3D printing technology, and community-led transformation, the Earthback Alliance is building homes that last centuries — from materials grown in the same communities that need them.

The Problem

Average distance construction materials travel

Global supply chains for conventional building materials are broken. Construction materials travel hundreds or thousands of miles, carrying embedded carbon and creating dependency on distant manufacturers. Conventional building methods are slow, materials are toxic, and homes deteriorate within decades. We need a fundamental shift: materials grown locally, construction powered by community, homes built to last centuries.

The Earthback Approach

Grow It. Build It. Own It.

Hemp Farming & Processing

Community-grown building materials with a 0–10 mile supply chain. Hemp sequesters carbon as it grows, and the entire plant becomes usable — fiber, shiv, and seed oil. Local processing creates jobs and keeps value within the community.

Layer-Free 3D Construction

Monolithic hempcrete printing eliminates the need for separate structural and insulation layers. Homes print in days instead of months. Strong, fireproof, and with R-values that exceed conventional construction — all from a single material.

Multi-Century Roofing

100+ year metal roofing with integrated solar. Durable infrastructure that outlasts the structural life of the home and generates clean power. Simple maintenance, zero degradation, and a pathway to energy autonomy for every household.

The Technology Supports, It Doesn't Require: Even though we've developed advanced systems — layer-free 3D printing, multi-century metal roofing, and integrated solar — none of this technology is required for an Earthback project to succeed. Every home can be built entirely with conventional tools and methods. We teach manual building first because real resilience comes from people, not machines.

The Structure

Three Organizations, One Mission

Earthback Foundation

Drives social impact through grants, education, and community development. Funds research, trains builders and farmers, and ensures the Earthback approach remains accessible to low-income communities worldwide.

Earthback Alliance

Coordinates partner networks across the supply chain. Connects farmers, processors, builders, and communities. Manages quality standards, knowledge sharing, and workforce development across all Earthback projects.

Earthback Project

Develops, tests, and deploys Earthback technology. Holds IP, licenses innovations, and manages the technology roadmap. Ensures the Alliance has access to cutting-edge tools and methods.

The Network

Where the Alliance Comes Together

The Earthback community platform is where builders, farmers, partners, and communities coordinate in real time. Share crop updates, track hempcrete cures, post wins and challenges, and learn from thousands of projects worldwide. One shared network. One shared mission.
