The Big Picture

Housing is Broken. We're Rebuilding It From the Ground Up.

The Problem

  • 1.8 billion people without adequate housing
  • 96,000 homes needed daily, globally
  • Conventional materials: toxic, fragile, reliant on fossil fuels
  • Supply chains broken — thousands of miles, no local control
  • Communities are left dependent on external builders and finance

The Earthback Answer

  • Community-grown hemp crops → local supply
  • 3D-printed hempcrete walls → monolithic strength, zero waste
  • Multi-century roofing → fire, flood, and rot resilience built in
  • Local economy loop → farmers, builders, communities profit directly
  • Carbon-negative homes → every home plants a future
"Earthback does not operate from a traditional profit motive. The only 'return' we care about is the success of the Alliance itself — communities gaining permanent skills, ownership, and economic sovereignty."
What We Build

Three Breakthrough Technologies

Layer-Free 3D Printing

Monolithic hempcrete walls printed without traditional layers. Stronger, faster, and with zero material waste. Each wall is load-bearing from the ground up.

Multi-Century Metal Roofing

100+ year service life, solar-ready, and fire-resilient. Engineered with Spengler Industries for durability that matches the lifespan of the home itself.

Integrated Solar Shingles

Modular solar systems that integrate seamlessly into roofing. Energy-positive homes with maintainable, replaceable components. No single point of failure.

"None of this technology is required for an Earthback project to succeed. Every home can be built with conventional tools. We teach manual building first because real resilience comes from people, not machines. Technology accelerates the work, but communities must always be able to build with their own hands."
